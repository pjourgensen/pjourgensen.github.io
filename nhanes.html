NHANES Analysis

Assessing Cancer Risk via Gradient-Boosted Decision Trees
PDF GitHub Repo

Abstact

Cancer prevention and risk prediction are among the foremost problems facing medical researchers today. While traditional correlational studies continue to persist, the future of cancer prevention, diagnosis, and treatment will heavily depend on machine learning methods applied to patient databases detailing symptoms, demographic information, family history, diet, lab results, and comorbidities (among other considerations). The National Health and Nutritional Examination Survey (NHANES) attempts to gather such data and add to its publicly accessible database each year. In this paper, I detail an approach to leveraging the NHANES data set to predict whether or not a patient will develop cancer at some point in their life. Features are initially selected based on common risk factors of non-communicable diseases and those indicated by current medical journals. They are then filtered and used to train a Gradient-Boosted Decision Tree model before final prediction. Further exploration and optimization exists in the feature selection and pre-processing steps, so this remains a living project.
