Heatwave Prediction

Longlead heatwave prediction of Eastern US via Convolutional LSTM Networks
GitHub Repo

Overview

Sea Surface Temperature (SST) is a valuable factor in climate modeling and weather forecasting. In 2016, Prof. Karen McKinnon published a paper on an approach she used to predict hot days of the Eastern US with lead times up to 50 days in advance. Upon reading the paper, I reached out to Prof. McKinnon to discuss the possibility of extending the research with the application of neural networks in an attempt to not only improve accuracy, but allow for generalized prediction across the world as well.

In this project, I attempt to apply neural networks to SST data for longlead predictions of heatwave events in the Eastern US. SST data is spatial in structure (Latitude x Longitude), which lends itself to convolutional analysis, while the dependencies of sequential days are temporal in structure, lending itself to time series analysis. The most common tools for these structures are Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for spatial reasoning and Long Short Term Memory (LSTM) Models for temporal reasoning. In 2015, Xingjian Shi et al. developed a model combining the 2 methods, that was then shown to have improved performance in spatiotemporal modeling.

In their model, they removed the fully connected networks in the input-to-state and state-to-state transitions and replaced with convolutional networks. Not only does this offer a reduction in free parameters come training time, but also offers the capability of learning spatiotemporal patterns within a single neural network layer. They aptly called these ConvLSTM layers.
